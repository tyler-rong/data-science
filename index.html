2020s: AI, Machine Learning, and Ethical Data Science

  • 2023-2024: Adoption of ethical AI guidelines globally.
  • 2021-2022: Rise of AutoML, making machine learning accessible to non-experts.
  • 2020: Expansion of cloud-based data science platforms, enhancing remote collaboration.

2010s: Data Science Boom and Technological Advancements

  • 2019: Significant advancements in natural language processing with models like GPT-3.
  • 2018: Widespread use of deep learning in industries like healthcare and automotive.
  • 2016-2017: Rapid growth in data science job market and educational programs.
  • 2015: The introduction of Apache Spark, improving upon Hadoop's capabilities.
  • 2014: Google's TensorFlow becomes a pivotal tool in machine learning.
  • 2012: Harvard Business Review labels data science as the "sexiest job of the 21st century."

2000s: Big Data and Advanced Analytical Techniques

  • 2008-2009: Big Data becomes a key concept in enterprise and scientific domains.
  • 2005-2007: Launch and development of Hadoop for big data processing.
  • Early 2000s: Proliferation of machine learning algorithms in predictive analytics.

1990s: Emergence of Digital Data and Data Mining

  • 1999: William S. Cleveland’s paper introducing the term "data science."
  • 1996: Popularization of the term "Data Mining."
  • 1991: Public access to the World Wide Web leads to an explosion of digital data.

1980s: Data Warehousing and Business Intelligence

  • 1989: BI tools become popular in corporate environments.
  • 1983: Introduction of the ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) process.
  • 1980: Emergence of data warehouse concepts.

1970s: Relational Databases and Statistical Software

  • 1977: SQL language development.
  • 1977: John Tukey’s Exploratory Data Analysis.
  • 1976: SAS (Statistical Analysis System) is launched.
  • 1974: Peter Naur used the term "Data Science."
  • 1970: Introduction of the relational database model by Edgar F. Codd.

1960s: Statistical Computing and Early Data Analysis

  • 1964: IBM System/360 popularizes computing in business.
  • 1964: Karen Spärck Jones published Synonymy and Semantic Classification.
  • 1962: John Tukey’s paper "The Future of Data Analysis" differentiates data analysis from statistical theories.

1940s: Early Computing and Statistical Theories
